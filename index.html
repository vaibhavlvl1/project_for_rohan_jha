About Me

Hello! I'm Rohan Jha, a rising 11th-grader at Cinco Ranch High School. I've always been passionate about solving problems, a journey that began with my fascination with math in elementary school and my desire to create impact to serve humanity. The complexity of numbers mesmerized me, and I spent weekends practicing to understand the mysteries of mathematics.

In middle school, I participated in math competitions like the AMC 8 & 10 and Mathcounts, which introduced me to problems that demanded creativity and out-of-the-box thinking. These experiences inspired my determination to tackle challenging problems and eventually led me to address real-world issues.

During a visit to India, I observed a significant gender disparity in educational opportunities for young girls. Many girls were unable to pursue schooling and were often forced into early marriage or household chores. This issue deeply impacted me, inspiring me to research the benefits of girls' education. My findings were published in a prestigious international journal namely Oxford Open Economics and US and Indian media.

I transformed my research into real-world impact by enhancing infrastructure, increasing the number of teachers, and driving social awareness in rural India. This led to the establishment of libraries serving 400+ students, the appointment of two additional teachers, and the construction of a new school building. I am actively expanding student engagement in libraries and social awareness through strategic initiatives, including a potential collaboration with Pratham USA.

Furthermore, I've also published research papers on topics such as carbon emissions reduction, data center carbon emissions and mitigation, predicting S&P 500, and improving profitability for cyclical businesses in notable journals like Frontiers in Sustainability, Intersect: The Stanford Journal of Science, Technology, and Society, and American journal of Student Research . These projects reflect my passion for economics and business and solving them using my interests in math and data science.

Outside of research and academics, I served as a VP for Mu Alpha Theta, coaching middle school students for math competitions , and enjoy sports and serving humanity. Feel free to reach out to me using the contact information provided at the end.

Research on Education, Sustainability, and Business

My research focuses on critical areas impacting humanity such as education in the developing world, sustainability, and business topics through data analytics, surveys, and interviews. My findings have been published in several journals.

Education

1. Transforming Girls’ Education through Social Awareness and Government Interventions: A Pathway to Equality

Authors : Rohan Jha, Rishabh Jha, and Mazhar Islam
Journal : Oxford Open Economics, Oxford University Press, 2025, vol. 4, pp. 1-19

Nobel Prize winners in Economics for 2014, 2015, and 2024 published a paper at Oxford Open Economics.

  • Full Paper Link

3. Assessment of US emissions targets and path forward using statistical modeling

Authors : Rohan Jha and Rishabh Jha
Journal : Intersect: The Stanford Journal of Science, Technology, and Society, 2024, vol. 18 (1)

  • Full Paper Link
  • Analyzed Biden administration's goal for a 50-52% reduction in greenhouse gases compared to 2005 by 2030 and carbon-free electricity by 2035.
  • Developed regression equations for CO2 emissions overall and also for power sector.
  • Found that the US is not on track to meet either of these two goals.
  • Identified how the US could meet these goals by using more renewable resources, electric vehicles, increasing the fuel efficiency of cars, and many more.

Library Establishment

Inauguration of the library at Middle School Fulbari, a school established in 1950, which has been serving around 1,000 families since its inception.

Inauguration of the library at Primary School Fulbari, Nai Tola, serving an area with a literacy rate of approximately 20%. The community has faced significant challenges in accessing educational resources until recently along with a lack of awareness.

Students reading books together inside the library of Middle School Fulbari.

Teacher issuing books inside the library

We established libraries in two schools: Middle School Fulbari and Primary School Nai Tola Fulbari, located in a rural Indian village namely Fulbari in the district of Araria. The aim is to foster curiosity, expand knowledge, and create new opportunities for students. These libraries house around 800 books covering literature, motivation, health, and foundational course materials with further addition of books, based on student input. They will benefit over 400 students and have been widely featured in local media, helping to raise social awareness.

Building and Teachers

Current situation of Primary School Nai Tola, Fulbari

Rohan and Rishabh meeting with the District Magistrate to discuss a building and the addition of two more teachers at Primary School Nai Tola, Fulbari.

Rohan and Rishabh with the District Education Officer are working to establish a new building and appoint two additional teachers at Primary School Nai Tola, Fulbari.

During our visit to Nai Tola School in Fulbari, we were shocked by its conditions—there was no school building, and the students were visibly uncomfortable. Determined to bring change, we raised the issue with the District Magistrate, who has now committed to providing a building by the end of 2025.
Furthermore, we found that just two teachers were responsible for educating 160 students across multiple grades. Acknowledging the immense challenge this posed for both teachers and students, we brought attention to the issue. As a result, plans are now underway to expand the teaching staff to four, ensuring improved educational support for the students.
