About Me

Hello! I'm Rohan Jha, a rising 11th-grader at Cinco Ranch High School. I've always been passionate about solving problems, a journey that began with my fascination for math in elementary school. The complexity of numbers mesmerized me, and I spent weekends practicing to understand the mysteries of mathematics.

In middle school, I participated in math competitions like the AMC 8 & 10 and Mathcounts, which introduced me to problems that demanded creativity and out-of-the-box thinking. These experiences inspired my determination to tackle challenging problems and eventually led me to address real-world issues.

During a visit to India, I observed a significant gender disparity in educational opportunities for young girls. Many girls were unable to pursue schooling and were often forced into early marriage or household chores. This issue deeply impacted me, inspiring me to research the benefits of girls' education. My findings were published in Indian media, which brought gender inequality to light, and prestigious international journals namely Oxford Open Economics .

Furthermore, I've also published papers on topics such as carbon emissions reduction, data center carbon emissions and mitigation, predicting S&P 500, and improving profitability for cyclical businesses in notable journals like Frontiers in Sustainability, Intersect: The Stanford Journal of Science, Technology, and Society, and American journal of Student Research . These projects reflect my passion for economics and business and solving them using my interests in math and data science.

Outside of research and academics, I served as a VP for Mu Alpha Theta , coaching middle school students for math competitions , and enjoy playing basketball, tennis, and listening to music. Feel free to reach out to me using the contact information provided below.

Research on Education, Sustainability, and Business

Library Establishment

Library inauguration at Middle School Fulbari. This school was established in 1950 and is serving 1000 families since the beginning.

Library inauguration at Primary School Fulbari, Nai Tola. This school is serving areas with a literacy rate of around 20%. They have struggled to have access to educational resources until very recently.

Students reading books together inside the library of Middle School Fulbari.

Teacher issuing books inside the library

We initiated libraries in two schools namely, Middle School Fulbari and Primary School Fulbari, Nai Tola. These schools are in a rural Indian village. It is expected to increase their curiosity, breadth of knowledge, and give them opportunities. These libraries have around 800 books across literature, self-help, health, and basic books helping in courses. This will serve ~400 students and was widely covered in local media, which also improves social awareness.

Building and Teachers

Current situation of Primary School Nai Tola, Fulbari

Rohan and Rishabh with District Magistrate to have a building and two more teachers at Primary School Nai Tola, Fulbari.

Rohan and Rishabh with District Education Officer to implement a building and two more teachers at Primary School Nai Tola, Fulbari.

While visiting Nai Tola school in Fulbari, we were shocked by the conditions of the school. There was no building for the school. The students were visibly uncomfortable, so we prompted change by telling the District Magistrate about this, and he has promised a building by the end of 2025.
Additionally, we discovered that only two teachers were responsible for educating 160 students across multiple grades. Recognizing the strain this placed on both the teachers and students, we highlighted this issue as well. As a result, plans are now in place to increase the teaching staff to four, ensuring better educational support for the students.
